# Workforce Attrition & Employee Insights Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using a dataset of 500 employee records. The objective is to identify key factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable business recommendations.

---

## Objectives

* Analyze employee attrition trends.
* Identify departments with higher turnover.
* Study the impact of overtime on attrition.
* Analyze the relationship between employee satisfaction and attrition.
* Compare attrition across different work modes.

---

## Tools Used

* Microsoft Excel
* Pivot Tables
* Data Visualization
* Business Analytics

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains 500 employee records with the following attributes:

* Employee ID
* Age
* Gender
* Department
* Education
* Experience
* Salary
* Work Mode
* Satisfaction Score
* Overtime
* Training Hours
* Performance Rating
* Promotion Status
* Attrition

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Department-wise Attrition

Finance department recorded the highest employee attrition with 22 employees leaving the organization.

### 2. Overtime Impact

Approximately 88% of employees who left were working overtime.

### 3. Employee Satisfaction

Around 85% of attrition cases occurred among employees with satisfaction scores between 1 and 4.

### 4. Work Mode Analysis

Office-based employees experienced the highest attrition compared to Hybrid and Remote employees.

---

## Recommendations

* Reduce excessive overtime.
* Improve employee engagement initiatives.
* Conduct retention reviews in Finance.
* Promote flexible work arrangements.
